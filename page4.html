AI can also be used to predict if future disasters may occur through some steps:

Past historical data, geospatial data, and weather data is used to identify patterns in areas where there is an increased risk of natural disasters. This data is collected from a variety of sources like satellites, sensors and previous reports.

The raw data has to be cleaned by removing any errors or irrelvent data, standardized in case of different units, selected for relevant data points, and integrated with multiple sources to get more views of potential disasters.

The AI is then trained using the data collected, and AI algorithms like neural networks learn to identify patterns and relationships in the data. Following this, simulations are run to test the AI and fix any issues that may occur.

The AI constantly monitors real-time data like changes in weather and seismic activity, and updates the data it has to generate new and more accurate predictions.
